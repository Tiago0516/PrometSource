¿What is the difference between SQL and NoSQL databases?

SQL

  • Use SQL when you need a structured database with clear relationships and consistent transactions.
  • Use it when you need to maintain complex relationships between data (e.g., related tables).
  • For applications where data integrity and consistency are crucial, such as banks, reservation systems, or ERPs.
  • When working with structured and well-defined data.

NoSQL

  • Use NoSQL when working with large volumes of unstructured or semi-structured data, or when you need high scalability and performance.
  • Use it when the data has no fixed structure or changes frequently.
  • For applications with massive scalability needs (e.g., social networks, big data analytics, IoT).
  • In systems with high read/write rates and low latency.
  • When the relationships between data are less important.
